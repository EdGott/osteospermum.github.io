Osteospermum is a genus of plants belonging to the Calenduleae family, with esquisite eyecatching flowers. It is one of the smaller groups in the Asteraceae family.

They are native to southern Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

The scientific name is derived from the Greek osteon (bone) and Latin spermum (seed). It has been given several common names: African daisy, South African daisy, Cape daisy and blue-eyed daisy.

The leaf form is lanceolate. The leaf margin is entire, but hardy types are toothed.
